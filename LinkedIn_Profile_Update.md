## Overview (the main "About" section)

AG Intellisense is the R&D company behind a new generation of high-value products extracted from agricultural waste.

We use metabolomics to map the molecular value locked inside agricultural waste streams — sugarcane bagasse, banana pseudostem, spent coffee grounds — then apply green chemistry to extract it cleanly and take it to market.

Our model is straightforward: characterise the waste stream, identify what's valuable, develop a green extraction process to food-grade or commercial-grade standards, and build it into a scalable product. We're currently focused on food-grade cellulose, with nanocellulose as our next product step — one of the fastest-growing advanced materials globally.

Based at the JCU Ideas Lab in Smithfield, Far North Queensland, we operate close to our feedstock sources and work in partnership with leading Australian research institutions.

We're building where the waste is produced — and turning what the industry discards into clean, high-value materials with real commercial applications and a genuine positive environmental impact.

## Services Section

**1. Waste Stream Metabolomics**
Untargeted metabolomics profiling of agricultural waste streams using HPLC, LC-MS, and GC-MS to map the full molecular profile and identify high-value compounds before extraction begins.

**2. Green Extraction Process Development**
Development of solvent-minimal, food-grade extraction and purification processes for cellulose and other target compounds — from laboratory proof-of-concept through to pilot scale.

**3. Product R&D & Commercialisation**
End-to-end product development from compound identification through process scale-up, designed for manufacturing at commercial scale and real market entry.
